Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 11, 2026

At MacPulse News Aggregator, we value the privacy of our visitors. This Privacy Policy document outlines the types of personal information that is received and collected by MacPulse and how it is used.

1. Information We Collect

MacPulse is a statically hosted aggregator. We do not maintain user databases, register accounts, or collect personal information.

  • Bookmarks: Any bookmarked news stories are stored entirely in your local browser storage (LocalStorage). This data is never sent to our servers, GitHub, or any third party.
  • Log Files: Like many other websites, we utilize basic web logs from our hosting platform (GitHub Pages). These logs include internet protocol (IP) addresses, browser type, Internet Service Provider (ISP), date/time stamp, referring/exit pages, and number of clicks. This data is not linked to any personally identifiable information.

2. Cookies and Web Beacons

MacPulse does not use cookies directly to track users. However, cookies are used by third-party advertising partners to deliver personalized advertising content based on your browsing history.

3. Advertising Partners Privacy Policies

Our site displays advertisements to monetize traffic. Third-party ad servers or ad networks use technologies like cookies, JavaScript, or Web Beacons that are sent directly to your browser when ads appear on the page. They automatically receive your IP address when this occurs.

These technologies are used to measure the effectiveness of their advertising campaigns and/or to personalize the advertising content that you see on websites that you visit.

Please note that MacPulse has no access to or control over these cookies that are used by third-party advertisers. You should consult the respective Privacy Policies of these third-party ad servers for more detailed information on their practices as well as for instructions about how to opt-out of certain practices.

4. Consent
